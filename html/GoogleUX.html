Certificate Case Studies

From November 2021 to June 2022

Overview

What I learn in this program

  • Follow the design process: empathize with users, define pain points, ideate solutions, create wireframes and prototypes, test and iterate on designs.

  • Understand the basics of UX research, like planning research studies, conducting interviews and usability studies, and synthesizing research results.

  • Apply foundational UX concepts, like user-centered design, accessibility, and equity-focused design.

  • Create a professional UX portfolio that includes 3 end-to-end projects: a mobile app, a responsive website, and a cross-platform experience.


Duration

Overall: 7 months.

From November 2021 to June 2022.

Projects Overviews


Mobile App

Project 1: The purpose of the "Arcade Preview Game" application is to assist users in discovering and saving their preferred arcade games. Users can create an account for convenient access to the tickets (points) they accumulate while enjoying their time at the arcade.


Responsive Website

Project 2: "Find Small Business" is a user-friendly website designed to help individuals locate nearby small businesses. By creating an account and joining our vibrant community, users can easily connect with local entrepreneurs.


Cross-Platform Experience

Project 3: The Learn How to Invest platform offers users a hassle-free way to learn about investing in their free time. It caters to young adults who may be unsure of where to begin their journey in understanding concepts such as investing, saving, retiring, and more.

The Problem & Solution

The Problem

Keeping up with new arcade games can be challenging for both new and experienced gamers alike.


The Solution

Create an app that enables users to effortlessly preview arcade games in advance.



The Problem

Finding local businesses or entrepreneurs nearby can be challenging, as there is currently no product or website that effectively addresses this issue.


The Solution

Create a brand-new website and devise a streamlined method for users to easily explore local small businesses in their vicinity.



The Problem

Finding a centralized resource for young adults to learn about investing can be challenging. Additionally, users often resort to utilizing various platforms for their learning needs.


The Solution

Create an app and website aiming to enhance education in areas like investing and related subjects, while assisting individuals in achieving their financial objectives.


User Research

Project 1:
Arcade Preview Game

I conducted interviews and developed empathy maps to gain insight into the user I am designing for and their needs. Through research, a primary user group identified was young adults who engage in arcade games. This user group confirmed initial assumptions regarding casual arcade gamers, but further investigation highlighted that knowledge alone was not the sole barrier for users to stay engaged with the games. Additional user challenges encompassed casual players, points system, and multiplayer games.

Project 2:
Find Small Business

To gain a deeper understanding I carried out interviews and developed empathy maps. Through extensive research, a primary user group emerged: individuals seeking to discover local businesses in their vicinity. This user group not only validated my initial assumptions about the users' desire to find local establishments but also uncovered their strong preference for a seamless and effortless experience.

Project 3:
Cross-Platform Experience

I create interview questions that were subsequently utilized in user interviews. Many participants in the interviews expressed limited to no familiarity with the concept of investing. The research feedback strongly indicated that users were receptive and eager to acquire knowledge in order to effectively utilize a user-friendly tool for investment guidance.

Take Aways

What I learned:

During the development of the Arcade Preview app, I discovered that initial ideas serve as merely the starting point. The incorporation of usability study feedback significantly influenced the app's design, resulting in an enhanced user experience.


What I learned:

I've discovered that simplicity plays a pivotal role in developing a robust search engine. The pivotal lesson I have learned from this journey is the undeniable significance of prioritizing user needs and conducting a comprehensive analysis of their pain points to elevate the overall design.

What I learned:

Through meticulous attention to each stage of the design process and a deep understanding of user requirements, I discovered that despite the initial challenge of creating a consistent user experience across both the app and website, I managed to develop solutions that were not only practical and valuable but also delivered a delightful experience.
